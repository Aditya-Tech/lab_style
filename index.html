Cats do not think that they are little people. They think that we are big cats.

Cat Names

  • A cat is a member of the family Felidae.
  • A group of kittens is called a "kindle."
  • A group of grown cats is called a "clowder."
  • There is no single cat called the panther. The name is commonly applied to the leopard, puma and the jaguar.

Cat's Eyes

  • A cat cannot see directly under its nose.
  • A cat is partially color blind. They have the equivalency of human red/green color blindness. (Reds appear green and greens appear red; or shades thereof.)
  • A cat does not see "detail" very well. To them, their person may appear hazy when standing in front of them.
  • A cat can see up to 120 feet away. Their peripheral vision is about 285 degrees.
  • A cat needs 1/6th the amount of light that humans do to see. Their night vision is amazing!

Cat's Facts

  • Domestic cats are essentially loners. When placed in a group, they develop their own hierarchy. As long as there is plenty of food on hand, a cat can learn to share it's domain with other cats.
  • Unlike humans and dogs, cats do not suffer a lot from loneliness. It is a mistake to project our social feelings onto our cats. They are social to a degree, but they are far more concerned with territorial issues than we can even imagine.
  • Cats in the US are said to have 9 lives whereas in Germany and southern Europe they only have 7 lives and the poor cats in Arabian countries are even only said to have 6 lives.
